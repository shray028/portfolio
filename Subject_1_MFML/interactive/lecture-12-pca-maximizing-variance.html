The Optimization Tradeoff

The visualization tracks two metrics:

  1. Retained Variance (Green): The spread of the green points. Goal: MAXIMIZE.
  2. Squared Error (Red): The sum of the areas of the red squares (Least Squares). Goal: MINIMIZE.
The Insight: Pythagorean Theorem

A² (Error) + B² (Retained Variance) = C² (Total Variance).

Since the Total Variance (C²) of the data is constant (the gray dashed line), maximizing the green bar automatically minimizes the red bar. They are the same optimization problem.

Why the Covariance Matrix?

The shape of the data cloud is defined by the Covariance Matrix. The direction that maximizes variance is the longest axis of this shape, which is mathematically found by calculating the first Eigenvector of the Covariance Matrix.
